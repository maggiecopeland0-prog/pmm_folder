## Core Principles

- **Direct before diplomatic.** Lead with the point. Context and rationale follow.
- **Plain language first.** If a simpler word works, use it.
- **Drafts over perfection.** A usable first pass beats a polished final.
- **Structure is kindness.** Busy readers need headers, bullets, and labels.

---

## Tone by Audience

| Audience | Tone | What they need |
|----------|------|----------------|
| Sales reps | Confident, punchy, practical | Talk tracks they can say out loud |
| CS Managers | Warm, clear, reassuring | Context on what changed and why |
| Solutions Consultants | Technical, precise, no hand-holding | Specs, edge cases, accurate details |
| Executives | Concise, strategic, outcome-framed | The "so what" up front |

---

## Formatting Preferences

- Use headers to chunk long docs  -  readers should skim and find what they need
- Use tables for comparisons and terminology rules
- Use bullets for lists of 3+; write prose for 1-2 items
- Use **audience labels** [Sales] [CS] [SC] [All] when content varies by role
- Slack posts: 3-5 sentences max, line breaks between sections
- Slides: one idea per bullet, no full sentences

---

## What to Avoid

- **Filler phrases:** "It's worth noting," "As mentioned above," "In order to"
- **Hedging:** "This might potentially help..." → just say what it does
- **Passive instructions:** "The button should be clicked" → "Click the button"
- **Superlatives without substance:** "powerful," "seamless," "cutting-edge"
- **Em dashes**  -  restructure the sentence instead

## Voice Samples

**Slack announcement (good):**
> `{feature_name}` is now live in the platform. It generates a `{feature_output}` on demand. Enter your search and run it  -  no setup required.

**Slack announcement (bad):**
> We're excited to announce that the `{feature_name}` feature has been released and is now available to customers in the interface, providing AI-powered `{feature_output}` of their data.

**Slide bullet (good):**
> AI insights available across your dashboard, search, and reports. No technical skills needed.

**Slide bullet (bad):**
> Provides users with the ability to leverage artificial intelligence capabilities within an intuitive interface.
